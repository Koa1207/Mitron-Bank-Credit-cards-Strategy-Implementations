Background

The Brief

Mitron Bank, a legacy financial institution headquartered in Hyderabad, wants to broaden its product offerings with a new line of credit cards. Before launching, the team needed clarity: who are the right customers, what do they spend on, and how do they prefer to pay?

A sample dataset of 4,000 customers across Mumbai, Delhi NCR, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, and Chennai was analysed — covering income levels, spending categories, payment method preferences, age and occupation.

"The goal was not to find the average customer — it was to find the right customer."

06 — Strategy

Five Recommendations

Based on the findings, five strategic levers can drive credit card adoption and usage across Mitron Bank's customer base.

01
Target IT Professionals & Freelancers, Age 25–45

Highest utilisation scores. Focus acquisition in Mumbai and Delhi NCR first with tailored messaging around bills and electronics rewards.

02
Category-Specific Reward Design

High cashback on Bills & Electronics reinforces existing habits. Double points on Groceries when paid by card drives UPI conversion.

03
"Switch & Save" Payment Campaigns

Target 21–24 and 35–45 groups showing high UPI/debit usage. Illustrate the tangible benefits of switching to credit card payment.

04
Geographic Rollout Strategy

Mumbai → aggressive acquisition. Delhi NCR → category promotions. Chennai → education-first campaigns to build trust before selling.

05
Segment-Specific Card Products

Lifestyle card for 21–24 (entertainment, online retail). Utility & travel card for 35–45. Essential-spend cashback card for 45+.

KPI
Success Metrics to Track

Credit card adoption rate by segment · Category-level spend shift · UPI-to-card conversion rate · City-wise activation volumes.

07 — Conclusion

The Takeaway

Credit card adoption potential is concentrated in a clear sweet spot: customers aged 25–45, in IT and freelance occupations, based in Mumbai and Delhi NCR. Bills and Electronics are already credit-card-friendly; Groceries represent the biggest conversion prize.

By building reward structures around these specific patterns — rather than generic offers — Mitron Bank can drive both immediate adoption and long-term loyalty. The data doesn't just tell us who to target; it tells us exactly why they'll say yes.
